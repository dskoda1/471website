  4. Rewrite the expressions a–d below in prefix notation. Assume the following precedences  and associativities for the operators:
    5. Operator
    		 Precedence
    (3 highest)
    		 Associativity
    ^
    		 3
    		 right
    *
    		 2
    		 left
    /
    		 2
    		 left
    +
    		 1
    		 left
    -
    		 1
    		 left
    and don't assume that any of the operators are commutative.
    A simple way to proceed is to perform the rewriting process in three steps:
    1. Parenthesize the expression fully, using the rules of precedence and associativity.
    2. Within each parenthesized subexpression, move the operator to left of its left operand.
    3. Remove the parentheses.
    You'll notice that in the resulting expression, the order of the operands is the same as in original- only the operators change positions.
    a)  w * x  +  y  *  z
    		      b)  w  * (x  /  y  +  z)
    c)  w  +  x  ^  y  -  z
    		      d)  w ^ x  ^  y  *  z
