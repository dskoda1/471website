Homework

Expect weekly assignments.  The purpose of the homework is to reinforce your understanding of the concepts presented in this course. THE PURPOSE OF DOING THE HOMEWORK IS TO LEARN, NOT GETTING A GOOD GRADE on the assignment.  Submission Requirements for all homeworks.

Homework assignments are individual efforts unless otherwise specified.  Homework turned in 1 day late will receive a 10% penalty, 2 days late 30% penalty.  

    Written assignments are to be typed and turned in by the due date on blackboard (tar or zip multipule files).  You may research solutions to all homework problems (programming and written).  ALL HELP/HINTS/SOLUTIONS must credited, otherwise it is considered cheating.  Remember the goal is to learn.  Remember you are responsible for the correctness of everything you write.  Failure to credit other students or sources can result in a zero on the homework.  

    Be clear, and neat in your write-ups. Understandability of the solutions is as necessary as correctness. Expect to lose points if you provide a badly written “correct” solution.  You are allowed to consult other books on homework, but you must cite your sources and write up your answers in such a way as to demonstrate that you understand the published solutions. Copying solutions without rewording and citing IS a violation of Thomas J. Watson School of Engineering and Applied Science Student Academic Honesty Code and can result in a zero for the assignment.  Repeated offenses will be handle as detailed in the Student Academic Honesty Code.    

    Homework will be graded not only for feed back to you but also to keep track of your attempts to keep up with the material.  Some answer will be cover during class and some may be posted on blackboard. 

    Programming assignments unless noted otherwise assignments are to be done individually. You may discuss homework problems, general strategies or algorithms with other students in the course, but you may not collaborate in the development of detail or actual writing of program, e.g. you may NOT work from written notes developed collaboratively. Students may not look at other students code for programs to be turned in. If you run into a programming problem PLEASE keep a balance between receiving help in debugging and doing your work.   It is your own responsibility to protect your work from unauthorized access. THE GOAL is for YOU to LEARN how to program.

    You should contact me (by email) or the ca if you have problems debugging programs.  The best way to learn a language is to program in it.  You must cite any found solutions. I don't want you to spend many hours debugging. You may have anyone look over your code to help you find simple syntax error. If the problem is deeper you should contact  the TA or me for help. Copying code IS a violation of Thomas J. Watson School of Engineering and Applied Science Student Academic Honesty Code and can result in a zero for the assignment. Repeated offenses will be handle as detailed in the Student Academic Honesty Code

    Programming assignment will be grade not to provide you with feedback but as a way to keep track of your attempts to learn the languages.  Solutions will be shown in class for you to copy down.  

    Much of what one learns in this course comes from trying to solve problems, so work hard on them. Doing a conscientious job on the homework is the best preparation for quizzes.

Grading Policy:

    Missing homework assignment may result in failure of this course.  See  submission rules for details about homework submission rules.

    Written Homework: 5%

    Late homework will not be accepted after the due date except by permission.   You are responsible for the correctness of your answers. You must attempt all homework assignments, failure to do so may result in zero for all homework and/or zero for quizzes. You may work in teams of 2, in which case you should turn on ONE copy of the assignment with both names and course sections on it.  

    Programming Assignments: 10%:

    Late homework will not be accepted after the due date except by prior permission.  All programs are an individual effort.  You are not to share code with other classmates until assignments are .  It is better to receive help with debugging code from the instructor or ta/ca.  There maybe some pass/fail programs that you will be allowed consult with other students.  You can not pass this course if you don't learn to program simple algorithms using the different languages paradigms presented in class.
     

    Labs and Presentation: 15%
    There will be no make ups.  You must be present at all labs. Presentation and research should be done in a team of 2-4 students.  Presentations should be between 20-30 minutes.  Written document 10-20 pages double space.  Members of the team should have limited experience with the topic.

    Tests : 70% 

    There will be 3 or 4 tests.  There will be a test during finals week.
     

    Academic dishonesty has no place in a university: It wastes our time and yours, and is unfair to the majority of students. Any violation of this code will be penalized and can lead to failure in the course. When in doubt, ask beforehand!
