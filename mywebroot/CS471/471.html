About

Catalog Description Introduction to the design and implementation of programming languages: linguistic features for expressing algorithms; formal syntax specification; introduction to language semantics and parsing; declarative programming (functional and goal-driven); scripting languages; imperative programming (procedural and object-oriented); comparative design and implementation issues across languages and paradigms. Assignments emphasize languages such as Prolog, Haskell, Python, and Ruby. Required Lab includes student presentations.
Prerequisites CS 375 and 373
Goals Develop computer science professionals who are knowledgeable consumers of programming languages, able to understand the strengths and limitations of languages and why the languages have them.
  • Have a firm grasp of different language features included in common languages such as C, C++,C#, Java, Haskell, Prolog and scripting languages; understand the benefits and drawbacks of these features.
  • Have a clear understanding of the different language paradigms, their benefits and drawbacks. Understand and appreciate the distinctions between imperative versus declarative languages; functional versus logic versus object-oriented languages.
  • Understand how various language features are implemented, with a view to better understand design choices and trade-offs in a language, and most importantly, become more effective programmers whatever language you choose to program in.
  • Acquire a good working knowledge of different programming styles and develop discipline in programming. Understand and use modern language features such as abstract data types, type-safe programming, inheritance, higher-order functions, etc.
